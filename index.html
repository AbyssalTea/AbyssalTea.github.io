About Me

I’m a graduate from the University of Wisconsin Madison, with Bachelor's in both Computer Engineering & Computer Science and certificates in both Game Design and German. I have hands-on experience in software developement, IT Support, and team leadership. I love designing and building games and interactive projects that are both functional and engaging. Whether focusing on public or personal projects, I aim to create games and tools that are intuitive, impactful, and fun to play.

Projects

Games

The Floor is Confusing

My first time working with VR in Unity, developed over three months with a team of four. The only way to move is by slingshotting the player in an arc — which made level design both fun and frustrating. I handled level layouts, respawning, checkpoint mechanics, and plenty of bug squashing.

Engine: Unity

Fleeing Footsteps

Made in two weeks as a personal challenge to blend Horror and Clicker genres in p5.js. The player has to alternate pressing the left and right arrow keys while a looming threat follows close behind.

Engine: p5.js & html

Link to Itch Page

Runic Ruin

My first try with PuzzleScript, which is mainly a puzzle engine, but I wanted to push it into story territory too. The biggest challenge I faced was splitting the game into two main areas: one where the player walks around, and another where puzzles are disguised as writing sigils in a book.

Engine: Puzzlescript

Link to Itch Page

Sleepless Shepherd

Built in about a week with the theme of making a “serious game.” I went with a sleep insomnia rhythm game where players count sheep crossing the screen while random distractions try to mess up their timing.

Engine: p5.js & html

Link to Itch Page

3D Scenes & Animations

Past Packages

Made in Maya over a month for a theme of Protection and Change. I modeled real-world items from my own life, animated them being packed into a box, had the box travel through different scenes, and slowly become battered to show the passage of time.

Practicing Pyromancer

This was my way of practicing nParticles and nMesh in Maya while also pushing myself to model and animate a character. The mage draws a fire symbol in a book, the fire appears, they quickly put it out, and flip the page, which allows the whole thing to loop seamlessly.

RenderCeption

My first time using Maya’s animation tools. I recreated my own workstation, and created a fake Maya scene running inside the computer. The camera moves around to show how perspective can hide or expose weird modeling mistakes.

A Darkening World

My first big Maya project where I took the theme of a vanitas and used it to recreate the bad ending of OneShot, which I’d just finished playing at the time.

Robot Character Study

Part of a class assignment focused on design. I mashed together real-world tech: running blades like legs, Wall-E–inspired hands, camera-like eyes, and an old satellite dish my family used to have. I even did a simple rig and dropped it into a scene from a past project for an extra render.
